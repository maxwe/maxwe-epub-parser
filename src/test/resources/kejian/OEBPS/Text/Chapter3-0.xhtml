Means of Transportation Unique in Different Parts of China

Foreigners traveling in different parts of China, can of course use conventional means of transportation. But it will be a most memorable experience to try some unique local means of transportation. The following are some unique local means of transportation.

Northeastern China: The pali or Chinese sled is the main means of transportation in the snowy forest area in the northeastern part of China. There are ox-drawn and horse-drawn sleds, but the dog-drawn sleds popular among the Hezhe ethnic minority people are the most unique. When winter comes, one can still take the horse-drawn vehicle for a ride on the icy river.

South of the Yangtze River: South of the Yangtze River where rivers criss-cross in a dense network, one can take a ferry boat to enjoy the beautiful scenery on both banks. The Shaoxing boatmen make an impressive sight as they row the black-awning-covered boats with their feet.

Southwestern China: One can use ferries or cableways to cross the rivers. If you are tired on the winding rugged mountain roads, you may hire the local people to carry you to the mountain by using huagan, a kind of Chinese-style sedan chair.

Beijing: Around Houhai, drivers will take you on a hutong tour pedaling pedicabs.
